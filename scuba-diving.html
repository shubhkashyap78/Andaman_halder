Why Choose Scuba Diving?

Scuba diving is the ultimate underwater adventure! Andaman's scuba sites are among India's finest, featuring pristine coral gardens, diverse marine life, and shipwrecks. Unlike snorkeling, diving allows you to explore depths of 18-20+ meters, accessing more marine wonders. Whether you're a certified diver or seeking certification, Andaman offers world-class training. The experience of breathing underwater, hovering in three dimensions, and encountering marine creatures up close is truly transformative. Each dive tells a different story—exploring vibrant reefs, encountering sea turtles, or investigating historical shipwrecks. It's an adventure that changes your perspective on the ocean forever.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need certification?+
For recreational dives, yes—you need PADI or equivalent certification. We offer Discover Scuba (1-2 hour intro) for non-certified beginners to experience diving safely. Full certification courses (Open Water, 3-4 days) are also available. Certified divers from any organization are welcome.
What certifications do you offer?+
We offer: Discover Scuba (intro), Open Water (certification), Advanced Open Water, Rescue Diver, and Divemaster. All taught by PADI-certified instructors. Courses combine classroom, confined water practice, and open water dives. We recognize certifications from PADI, NAUI, SSI, and other major agencies.
Is diving safe?+
Scuba diving is statistically very safe when following proper procedures. Our instructors are highly trained, equipment is regularly serviced, and we follow all international dive safety standards. We conduct pre-dive briefings, maintain small group sizes, and perform safety checks on all gear.
What marine life will we see?+
Andaman dive sites feature: manta rays, sea turtles, reef sharks, groupers, barracudas, fusiliers, over 200 coral species, and countless tropical fish. Site-specific encounters vary—some areas feature shipwrecks, deep reefs, or macroinvertebrates. October-May offers the best visibility and marine life encounters.
What's the best depth for beginners?+
Beginners typically dive to 12-18 meters. As you gain experience and certification, depths increase up to 40 meters (Advanced). Our instructors choose sites and depths appropriate for each diver's skill level. Shallow reefs (5-12m) are perfect for learning while offering incredible marine biodiversity.
How physically demanding is diving?+
Moderate fitness is recommended but not required. Most physical effort comes from carrying equipment (we help!) and entry/exit. Underwater, buoyancy control means minimal physical exertion. Anyone with heart conditions, uncontrolled diabetes, or severe joint problems should consult a doctor first.
